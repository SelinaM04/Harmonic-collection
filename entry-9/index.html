What is Ashtanga Yoga?

Ashtanga Yoga is a dynamic and physically demanding form of yoga, renowned for its focus on strength, flexibility, and breath control. Originating from the teachings of the legendary Sri K. Pattabhi Jois, this method involves a set sequence of postures linked together with a vinyasa flow. Ashtanga Yoga is divided into six series, each increasing in difficulty, designed to purify the body and mind.

Practitioners of Ashtanga Yoga synchronize breath with movements in a process called "vinyasa," to produce internal heat designed to purify the body. This yoga style is known for its emphasis on consistency and development through a defined progression of poses. Ashtanga's rigorous approach makes it both a challenging and rewarding practice, appealing to those who appreciate a structured and disciplined approach to yoga.

What Makes Ashtanga Yoga Special?

Ashtanga Yoga stands out as a highly structured and physically demanding form of yoga, known for its ability to build strength, flexibility, and mental focus. Here are some key aspects that make Ashtanga Yoga special:

  • Fixed Sequence: Unlike many other yoga styles, Ashtanga follows a precise sequence of poses. Practitioners move through a series of flows, each with a specific order and method, ensuring a comprehensive workout of the entire body.
  • Tristhana: This refers to the three places of attention or action: posture, breathing system, and looking place. These three are key to the Ashtanga Yoga method and are used to purify the body, nervous system, and mind.
  • Ujjayi Breathing: This specific type of breath control is integral to Ashtanga Yoga. Ujjayi Breathing is a method of breathing through the nose, which helps to concentrate the mind and generate internal body heat.
  • Bandhas: Internal body locks, known as Bandhas, are crucial to the practice. They help support the body in the poses, direct the flow of energy, and enhance concentration.
  • Mysore Style: Ashtanga is often taught in Mysore style, where each student practices at their own pace while the teacher provides individual guidance. This method allows for a highly personalized approach to yoga.
  • Discipline and Regular Practice: Ashtanga encourages consistent practice, ideally six days a week. This discipline is believed to deepen the physical and mental aspects of yoga, leading to significant personal growth and transformation.

Ashtanga's blend of dynamic movement, controlled breathing, and focused mind work synergistically to create a meditative and physically challenging practice. This makes it particularly appealing to those who seek a holistic approach to physical and mental wellness.
