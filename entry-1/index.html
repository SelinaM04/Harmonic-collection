The Origin of Yoga

Yoga has been practiced for more than 5,000 years since its origin. Archaeological excavations have proved that yoga was practiced in the Indus Valley civilization around 3000 BC. Among the relics unearthed at Mohenjo-Daro and his sister city Harappa were seals with images of gods sitting in meditation. At that time, the Aryans entered the Indus Valley and, in conjunction with the local natives, absorbed and passed on yoga as one of the key disciplines of their religious practice.

Contemporary philosophical researchers and yoga scholars, based on evidence and legends, imagined and described the process of the emergence of yoga: on the side of the Himalayas, there is a mountain as high as 8,000 meters high as the Mother of God, where there are many hermits, who, through meditation and austerity, many cultivated into saints, and began to be envied by a portion of the population to follow them, and these saints in the form of the secret method of cultivation will be passed on to the followers, which are the These sages taught their followers the secrets of their practice in the form of mantras, and this was the original Yoga practitioners.

The early practitioners of Yoga were ascetics who challenged nature at the foot of the snow-covered Himalayas all year round. To live a long and healthy life, they had to face "disease", "death", "body", "soul" and the relationship between man and the universe. At the same time, they analyzed how the mind influences health, explored means of controlling the mind, and pursued ways to harmonize the body, mind, and nature, thus developing human potential, wisdom, and spirituality, which is the origin of Yoga Meditation. In the beginning, yoga practitioners were confined to Himalayan caves and the heart of dense forests, and later expanded to monasteries and country houses. When yoga practitioners enter the deepest level in deep meditation, they will get the combination of individual consciousness and cosmic consciousness, awaken the inner sleeping energy, get enlightenment and the greatest pleasure, which makes yoga has a strong vitality and attraction, and gradually spread among ordinary people in India.

Why more and more people are practicing yoga

  1. Many people are drawn to yoga for the physical advantages. It can improve flexibility, strength, posture, and balance. Plus, it's a great workout without the high-impact stress on joints that some other exercises can cause.
  2. Let's face it, life can get hectic. Yoga emphasizes relaxation and meditation, which can help reduce stress and promote a more peaceful state of mind. The deep breathing exercises in particular can be super chill.
  3. Beyond just stress relief, yoga can also benefit those dealing with anxiety, depression, and other mental health issues. The mindfulness and meditation aspects of yoga encourage practitioners to stay present, which can be a huge help for mental well-being.
  4. Some folks have found relief from chronic pain issues by practicing yoga. It's all about improving body alignment, strength, and flexibility.
  5. Yoga isn't just about poses; it's a whole philosophy. The principles of yoga, like non-violence, truth, and contentment, can offer guidance in daily life.
